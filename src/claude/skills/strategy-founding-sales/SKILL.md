## When to Use This Skill

Load this skill when working on:
- Building or refining an early-stage sales process (pre-sales-hire)
- Crafting outbound messaging (cold emails, LinkedIn, follow-ups)
- Preparing for or debriefing discovery/demo calls
- Defining ICP, personas, or buyer segmentation
- Designing pricing and packaging for initial customers
- Building pipeline tracking and CRM workflows
- Transitioning from founder-led sales to a hired sales team

## Founding Sales Mindset

Founding sales is not scaled sales. The goal is learning, not efficiency.

| Scaled Sales | Founding Sales |
|-------------|----------------|
| Optimize conversion rates | Discover what converts and why |
| Delegate to reps | Founder is the rep |
| Playbook execution | Playbook creation |
| Pipeline volume | Signal density per conversation |
| Win rate metrics | Pattern recognition across losses |
| CRM hygiene | Obsessive note-taking |

### Core Principles

- **Talk to customers before building** — sales conversations are the cheapest form of product research
- **Sell the problem, not the product** — if the prospect doesn't feel the pain, no feature list will close them
- **Disqualify fast** — your scarcest resource is founder time, not leads
- **Price on value, not cost** — early customers pay for outcomes, not features
- **Every "no" is data** — document why deals die with the same rigor as why they close
- **Do things that don't scale** — manual onboarding, custom demos, personal follow-ups

## Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

### ICP Definition Framework

| Dimension | Questions to Answer |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Company size | Revenue range, employee count, growth stage |
| Industry/vertical | Which verticals have the most acute pain? |
| Tech stack | What tools do they already use? What integrates? |
| Buying trigger | What event makes them start looking for a solution? |
| Budget authority | Who controls the budget? What's the typical spend? |
| Timeline | How urgent is their need? What's driving urgency? |
| Anti-ICP | Who should you explicitly NOT sell to? |

### ICP Validation Signals

| Strong Signal | Weak Signal |
|--------------|-------------|
| They have budget allocated for this problem | "We might have budget next quarter" |
| They've tried solving it with internal tools | They haven't thought about the problem |
| Decision-maker is in the conversation | "I'll need to run this by my boss" |
| They can articulate the cost of inaction | They're "exploring options" |
| They have a timeline for implementation | "No rush, just researching" |

### Persona Mapping

```
Primary Buyer: [Title] — the person who signs the contract
  Pain: [What keeps them up at night]
  Metric: [What number they're measured on]
  Objection: [Their default reason to say no]

Champion: [Title] — the person who advocates internally
  Pain: [Day-to-day frustration they experience]
  Metric: [What makes their job easier]
  Motivation: [Why they'd push for this internally]

Blocker: [Title] — the person who can kill the deal
  Concern: [Security, compliance, budget, integration]
  Need: [What they require to not block]
```

## Outbound Prospecting

### Cold Email Framework

| Element | Rule |
|---------|------|
| Subject line | 3-6 words, lowercase, no clickbait, references their world |
| Opening line | About THEM, not you — reference something specific |
| Pain statement | One sentence describing the problem you solve |
| Credibility | One proof point (customer name, metric, relevant experience) |
| CTA | One low-friction ask (15-min call, not "let me show you our platform") |
| Length | Under 100 words total |

### Cold Email Template

```
Subject: [specific pain point] at [company]

Hi [First Name],

[Observation about their company/role — shows you did research].

[One sentence: the problem you solve, framed as their pain].

[One proof point: "We helped [similar company] achieve [specific result]"].

Worth a 15-minute call to see if this is relevant for [Company]?

[Your name]
```

### Follow-Up Cadence

| Touch | Timing | Channel | Approach |
|-------|--------|---------|----------|
| 1 | Day 0 | Email | Initial cold email |
| 2 | Day 3 | LinkedIn | Connect + short note referencing email |
| 3 | Day 7 | Email | New angle or value-add (article, insight, data point) |
| 4 | Day 14 | Email | Social proof or case study relevant to their vertical |
| 5 | Day 21 | Email | Breakup email ("Closing the loop — is this a priority?") |

### LinkedIn Outreach

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Personalize the connection request (under 300 chars) | Send the default "I'd like to connect" |
| Reference their content, company news, or mutual connections | Lead with your product pitch |
| Keep messages to 2-3 sentences | Write multi-paragraph walls of text |
| Engage with their posts before reaching out | Cold pitch in the connection request |
| Move to email or call quickly | Have long sales conversations in LinkedIn DMs |

## Discovery Calls

### Pre-Call Research Checklist

- Company website, recent news, funding announcements
- Prospect's LinkedIn (role, tenure, career path, posts)
- Current tech stack (check job postings, BuiltWith, LinkedIn)
- Competitors they might be evaluating
- Mutual connections who can provide context

### Discovery Question Framework

| Category | Questions |
|----------|-----------|
| Situation | "Walk me through how you currently handle [process]." |
| | "How many people are involved in [workflow]?" |
| | "What tools are you using for this today?" |
| Problem | "What's the biggest challenge with that approach?" |
| | "When this breaks down, what happens?" |
| | "How much time/money does that cost you per [period]?" |
| Implication | "If you don't solve this in the next 6 months, what happens?" |
| | "How does this affect [their team / their customers / their boss's priorities]?" |
| Need-Payoff | "If you could wave a magic wand, what would the ideal look like?" |
| | "What would solving this be worth to you annually?" |

### Call Structure (30 min)

| Phase | Time | Purpose |
|-------|------|---------|
| Rapport + Agenda | 2 min | "Here's what I was thinking for our time — does that work?" |
| Situation questions | 5 min | Understand current state |
| Problem questions | 8 min | Uncover pain and quantify impact |
| Implication questions | 5 min | Amplify urgency |
| Demo / Solution fit | 7 min | Show only what maps to their stated pain |
| Next steps | 3 min | Agree on concrete next action with a date |

### Discovery Anti-Patterns

| Anti-Pattern | Fix |
|-------------|-----|
| Pitching before understanding pain | Ask 3+ problem questions before showing anything |
| Showing every feature | Demo only the 2-3 features that map to stated pain |
| Accepting "sounds interesting" as positive signal | Push for specifics: "Interesting how? Which part?" |
| Ending without next steps | Always book the next meeting before hanging up |
| Talking more than 40% of the time | If you're talking more than listening, stop and ask a question |
| Not taking notes | Write down exact quotes — they're gold for follow-ups and marketing |

## Objection Handling

### Framework: Acknowledge, Clarify, Respond

```
1. Acknowledge: "That makes sense" / "I hear that a lot"
2. Clarify: "Help me understand — is it [specific interpretation]?"
3. Respond: Address the real objection, not the surface one
```

### Common Objections

| Objection | Real Concern | Response Pattern |
|-----------|-------------|-----------------|
| "Too expensive" | Value not established or wrong buyer | "Compared to what? What's the cost of doing nothing?" |
| "We're using [competitor]" | Switching cost feels high | "What made you choose them? What's working? What isn't?" |
| "Not a priority right now" | No urgency or wrong timing | "What would need to change for this to become a priority?" |
| "Need to talk to my team" | You're not talking to the decision-maker | "Happy to join that conversation — who else should be involved?" |
| "We'll build it in-house" | Underestimating cost/time to build | "How long would that take? What's the opportunity cost of that engineering time?" |
| "Send me more info" | Polite brush-off | "Happy to — what specific questions should I make sure to answer?" |
| "We had a bad experience with [similar tool]" | Trust deficit | "What went wrong? How would you evaluate differently this time?" |

### Pricing Objections Specifically

| Signal | Interpretation | Response |
|--------|---------------|----------|
| "Too expensive" before seeing value | You pitched too early | Back up, re-establish pain and cost of status quo |
| "Too expensive" after a good demo | Price anchoring issue | Reframe to ROI: "If this saves X hours/week at $Y/hour..." |
| "Can you do a discount?" | They want to buy, negotiating | Trade value for discount: longer commitment, case study, referral |
| Silence after pricing | Processing or sticker shock | Wait. Don't fill the silence. Let them respond first. |

## Pipeline Management

### Deal Stages

| Stage | Definition | Exit Criteria |
|-------|-----------|---------------|
| Lead | Name + company, no conversation yet | Responded to outreach |
| Discovery | Had initial conversation | Pain confirmed, next meeting booked |
| Evaluation | Actively evaluating your solution | Demo completed, stakeholders identified |
| Proposal | Commercial terms shared | Proposal reviewed by decision-maker |
| Negotiation | Terms being discussed | Verbal agreement on terms |
| Closed Won | Contract signed | Payment received or contract executed |
| Closed Lost | Deal is dead | Loss reason documented |

### Pipeline Hygiene

| Rule | Frequency |
|------|-----------|
| Review every open deal | Weekly |
| Update deal stage and next action | After every interaction |
| Kill stale deals (no activity 2+ weeks) | Biweekly |
| Document loss reasons in detail | Immediately on close-lost |
| Review loss reasons for patterns | Monthly |

### Key Metrics (Early Stage)

| Metric | What It Tells You |
|--------|------------------|
| Conversations per week | Are you generating enough at-bats? |
| Discovery-to-demo conversion | Is your ICP definition right? |
| Demo-to-proposal conversion | Is your product solving real pain? |
| Average deal cycle length | How long should you expect to wait? |
| Loss reasons by category | Where is the process breaking down? |
| Revenue per customer | Is your pricing right? |
| CAC (your time as cost) | Is this sales motion sustainable? |

## Pricing Strategy

### Early-Stage Pricing Principles

- **Start higher than you think** — it's easier to discount than to raise prices
- **Price on value, not cost** — what is the outcome worth to the customer?
- **Keep it simple** — 2-3 tiers max, no complicated usage-based pricing early on
- **Annual contracts preferred** — reduces churn, improves cash flow, signals commitment
- **Grandfather early customers** — reward early adopters but don't lock yourself into unsustainable pricing

### Pricing Discovery Questions

| Question | Purpose |
|----------|---------|
| "What are you paying for [current solution]?" | Anchors to existing spend |
| "What would solving this problem be worth per year?" | Establishes value ceiling |
| "What's your budget for tools in this category?" | Reveals budget constraints |
| "How do you typically evaluate ROI on tools like this?" | Understand their value framework |

### Pricing Tier Structure

```
Starter: Entry point, solves core pain, limited scale
  Target: small teams, initial adoption
  Goal: low friction, prove value

Growth: Full feature set, team collaboration, integrations
  Target: scaling teams, power users
  Goal: capture majority of revenue

Enterprise: Custom, security, compliance, SLAs, dedicated support
  Target: large orgs, regulated industries
  Goal: maximize deal size with high-touch sales
```

## Founder-Led Sales Transition

### Signs You're Ready to Hire

- Repeatable pattern: same ICP, same pitch, same objections, predictable close rate
- You can articulate the sales process in a document a new hire could follow
- Pipeline exceeds what one person can manage
- You're turning down meetings due to time constraints
- Win rate is stable (not still wildly fluctuating)

### What to Document Before Hiring

| Document | Contents |
|----------|----------|
| ICP definition | Firmographic + behavioral criteria with examples |
| Outbound playbook | Email templates, cadence, channel strategy |
| Discovery script | Questions, talk tracks, qualification criteria |
| Demo flow | What to show, in what order, for each persona |
| Objection handling guide | Top 10 objections with proven responses |
| Pricing guide | Tiers, discounting rules, approval thresholds |
| Competitive positioning | Key differentiators vs top 3 competitors |
| Loss analysis | Why deals die, patterns, and what to avoid |

### First Sales Hire Profile

| Trait | Why |
|-------|-----|
| Early-stage experience | They can handle ambiguity and lack of process |
| Builder mentality | They'll improve the playbook, not just execute it |
| Consultative style | Enterprise or mid-market background, not transactional |
| Technical fluency | Can speak credibly to your buyer's domain |
| Low ego | Will take feedback and iterate, not "I know how to sell" |

## Sales Collateral

### One-Pager Structure

```
Headline: [Outcome you deliver] for [who]
Subhead: [One sentence describing how]

Problem (3 bullets):
- [Pain point 1 with quantified impact]
- [Pain point 2 with quantified impact]
- [Pain point 3 with quantified impact]

Solution (3 bullets):
- [Capability 1 -> outcome]
- [Capability 2 -> outcome]
- [Capability 3 -> outcome]

Social Proof:
- [Customer quote or metric]
- [Logo bar or customer count]

CTA: [Single clear next step]
```

### Case Study Structure

```
Customer: [Name, industry, size]
Challenge: [What problem they had, quantified]
Solution: [What you did, specifically]
Results: [3 metrics showing improvement]
Quote: [Customer's words about the experience]
```
